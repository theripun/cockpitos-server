PART 2: THE SOLUTION (02:30 – 04:30)



4



VISUAL: Show the Cockpit Desktop https://cockpit.ripun.site/home. It looks like macOS or Windows, but in a browser. Mouse moving smoothly, windows opening. The wallpaper is dynamic and beautiful.



NARRATOR: Introducing Cockpit. 

Cockpit is the world’s first true Operating Surface for the cloud. It’s not a dashboard. It’s not a control panel with a thousand checkboxes. It is a full, desktop-class experience that turns any server, anywhere in the world, into a computer you can see, touch, and use just like a usual desktop personal computer.



5



VISUAL: Show the “One Line Install” process https://cockpit.ripun.site (Configure your PC) - phase. A user enters the fields of inputs, pastes it, and the desktop config appears. The “Connect Device” button and right side setup terminal fills up quickly.



NARRATOR: And the best part? 

It works with the infrastructure you already have. You don’t need to buy special hardware. You don’t need to migrate your data. You just enter the necessary information into input fields and click Connect Device and done. Rest we will handle it effectively “installing” the cockpit operating surface onto your server. Usually in a good network connection it takes around 4 to 5 minutes for the initial setup. And your Cloud Computer is ready.



6



VISUAL: Close up on the “Connect Device” button turning with a checkbox, and the desktop config setup fading out. A “Home Page” widget appears.



NARRATOR: Suddenly, that remote machine thousands of miles away feels like it’s sitting right in front of you. It’s ready to work. It’s responsive. It’s yours.






PART 3: THE EXPERIENCE (04:30 – 10:30)



7



VISUAL: The narrator (or user) sitting at a laptop. They open a browser tab. The Cockpit desktop loads instantly.



NARRATOR: Let me show you what using a “Cloud Computer” actually feels like. 

You open your browser. That’s it. No heavy software to install. You’re greeted by a familiar lock screen. You enter your PIN, and you’re in.



8



VISUAL: Montage of the UI features. The Dock, the Menu Bar, the Window controls. Emphasize the “Glass” aesthetic and smoothness.



NARRATOR: Look at this. It feels familiar, doesn’t it? 

That’s by design. You have a Dock with your apps. A Menu Bar with your system status - Wi-Fi, battery, time. Windows snap, drag, and resize exactly how you expect. It has widgets such as realtime time location weather, your system booster button, and a notepad for your use. 

Use it for five seconds, and you already know how to use it. There is zero learning curve. 



9



VISUAL: Opening “Terminal” app. Typing “hello” or running a simple script to show it’s real.



NARRATOR: And for the experts who still want that raw power? 

The Terminal is still there. But now, it lives in a beautiful, multi-tabbed window. 

You can run your scripts, monitor your logs, and do your deep work - but with all the comforts of a modern interface around you. It’s the best of both worlds.



10



VISUAL: Opening the “File Explorer” app. Navigating folders, opening a document, editing it, and saving.



NARRATOR: Now let’s look at Files Explorer. 

On a traditional server, moving a file means typing commands. If you make a typo, you could delete everything. And it’s stressful.

Here? You just open the File Explorer. You can see your folders, files, everything. 

You can drag and drop to move things. You can double-click a text file to edit it right here. You can even view photos and play videos.

It’s your server’s hard drive, made visual. It transforms a database of text into a living library of your work.



07:30



VISUAL: Opening the “Task Monitor”. Showing the CPU graphs and the “Boost” button.



NARRATOR: Now, let’s talk about performance. This is the Task Monitor. 

Instead of cryptic text logs, you get beautiful, real-time graphs. You can see exactly how hard your “Cloud Computer” is working. See a program that’s stuck? Just click it and hit “End Task.” Need to clean up? Hit the Booster button. It sweeps through your system, cleans out the junk, and optimizes performance-all in one click. It’s like having a dedicated IT technician built right into the interface.









VISUAL: Opening "Settings". Showing the "Connectivity" tab and "Firewall" toggle. Then switching to the "Gallery" app showing photos.



NARRATOR: We’ve also reimagined security and media. Configuring a firewall usually requires a degree in networking. In Cockpit, it's just a toggle in Settings Settings.
